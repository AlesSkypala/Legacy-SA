 Transfer rate
Transfer rate over the entire data collection on the disk array ports. X-Axis represents Date-Time, Y-Axis represents transfer rate on ports [MB/s]. Average: %PortMBsAvg%, peak: %PortMBsMax%.

The Write Block Size histogram provide insight into the distribution of Write IO requests size. X-Axis represents latency, Y-Axis represents Write IO size rate [%].


 Read/Write and Random/Sequential ratio
Read/Write ratio shows the ratio of read activity to write activity over the entire data collection. X-Axis represents Date-Time, Y-Axis represents Read/Write ratio [%].

Random/Sequential ratio shows the ratio of Random IOPs and Sequential IOPs over the entire data collection. X-Axis represents Date-Time, Y-Axis represents Read/Write ratio [%].
Average Read/Write ratio and Random/Sequential ratio.


 Utilization of the physical disks
Utilization of the physical disks over the entire data collection. X-Axis represents Date-Time, Y-Axis represents drive utilization [%]. Average: %PGUtilAvg%, peak: %PGUtilMax%.

Utilization of physical disks over the Parity Groups. X-Axis represents Parity Groups, Y-Axis represents drive utilization [%].


 Utilization of the Storage procesors and Write pending rate
Utilization of the Storage procesors over the entire data collection. X-Axis represents Date-Time, Y-Axis represents CPU utilization [%]. Average: %ProcUtilAvg%. peaks: %ProcUtilMax%.

Utilization of Storage procesors over the cores. X-Axis represents Cores, Y-Axis represents Cores utilization [%]


The write pending rate indicates the quantity of cache alocated by write operations. X-Axis represents Date-Time, Y-Axis represents Wite pending rate [%]. Average: %WritePendingAvg%, peaks: %WritePendingMax%.
This metrics is critical for the latency: <33% Normal value, >33% Hosts priority is decreased, >70% Host are paused until cache destage.


 FrontEnd/Backed ratio
The number of total read and write I/O operations per second over the entire data collection period. Total Back-end I/O may be compared to Total I/O and the difference is due to the effects of the array cache according to RAID type. X-Axis represents Date-Time, Y-Axis represents FE-BE IOPs.
